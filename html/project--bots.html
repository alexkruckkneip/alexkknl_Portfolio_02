Introduction

Bots have been the biggest innovation to reduce costs for Genesys customers in the call center service industry. The problem was that in 2019, bot authoring software could only be operated by highly trained engineers. After I started in March 2019, Genesys wanted to sell a fully-featured bot authoring software by the end of the year that would be easy to use for almost anyone.

Senior stakeholders did not really believe in the success of the product as our competitors such as AWS and IBM had already successfully launched similar applications in the market for quite some time.

In this case study, I tell the story of how a group of courageous product people and I persisted and challenged the senior sales team from the US.

Problem

Problem

Only for pro devs

The UI design shown here was the result of a series of meetings and workshops with representatives from a wide range of disciplines. The language, data, AI and information scientists were spread across three different continents. Our first focus was on defining all the tasks that need to be completed so that a conversation bot can be created.

A competitor copy

Everyone involved probably already had solutions from competitors in mind. Because the focus was on user flow and UX, we used a standard UI kit for these initial UI designs. In this combination, what we had created up to this point also looked like established products on the market. Our Genesys unique selling point was missing.

How could we go beyond a mere functional copy? Who do we actually want to reach with a better product? Let's find out!

Users & mission

Users & mission

Kevin

What if even simple office workers who handle calls could create a bot? Maybe you saw Ghostbusters 2016? Have you perhaps noticed Kevin Beckman, played by Chris Hemsworth, as the new secretary of the Ghostbusters? The brilliant product manager Jonathan McKenzie had the great idea for a very special persona mission and user story

Who you gonna call?

In our story, Kevin from the Ghostbusters has the brilliant idea to make his life a little easier. Our Genesys Click and Go bot is designed to help him with this. In the best case scenario, it's so easy that he can also provide telephone service for the new Ghostbusters branches in Los Angeles and San Francisco.
