Business objective

Rentalcars.com had a very clear main KPI (Key Performance Indicator), increasing BPD (Bookings Per Day). When I joined Rentalcars in Manchester in 2015, a highly efficient A/B experiment platform was the key driver for success.

On this page I present some of my successful AB experiments and describe what may have contributed to their success.

Ideation & experiment process

AB experiment process

1. Hypothesis

Any idea that someone wanted to test in an experiment had to be formulated in the form of a hypothesis. What problem can the proposed change solve? How can success be measured?

As a rule, at least the last part of the following formulation was the same.

“If we change this, we will achieve this effect. We can measure success by increasing conversion.“

2. Voting

We met every two weeks for an idea session. When I started at Rentalcars, usually only the interdisciplinary core team was invited.

Everyone was given about 3 minutes to present their idea in hypothesis form. The hypotheses were then written on a whiteboard in the form of sticky notes. Each participant then had 2 minutes to cast their vote in the form of three red dots.

Finally, the team discussed how many of the hypotheses with the most votes could be planned for the next sprint. The product owner had the final say

3. Prototyping

As a rule, the changes that had to be made to prepare the experiment were rather small. That's why extensive prototypes created with Adobe XD or Sketch were the exception. Lo-fi mockups with pencil and paper were absolutely sufficient.

My actual task was to prepare the graphically more complex experiments in code. I then put together small closed packages of CSS, JS and HTML code without dependencies on other libraries. I then handed this over to the developers.

4. AB Test

Our experiments were published with other experiments on the self-built Rentalcars AB Test Platform. The proportion of traffic to be redirected to the experiment was decided depending on the type and possible risk. An online dashboard allowed live monitoring of how much more or fewer bookings an experiment caused.

There are three different categories of experiment performance. The red failed ones that generated fewer than 90 more bookings per day In a green experiment that generated between 90 and 95 additional bookings, the PO was responsible for deciding whether to activate.

Gold experimenting with over 95 bookings per day was the winning ticket. When I started, only between 5% and 7% of the experiments were in this category.

Good experiments had to be monitored for about two weeks before they were fully activated.
